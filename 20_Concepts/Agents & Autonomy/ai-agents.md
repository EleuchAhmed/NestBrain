# Ai-Agents

## Definition
**Ai-Agents** are autonomous or semi-autonomous software entities that perform tasks, make decisions, or interact with environments on behalf of users or systems. They leverage artificial intelligence (AI) techniques, such as machine learning, natural language processing, or reinforcement learning, to adapt, learn, and operate with varying degrees of independence.

## Key Details
- **Autonomy**: Can execute tasks without constant human oversight, ranging from simple rule-based actions to complex decision-making.
- **Applications**: Used in customer service (chatbots), automation (robotic process automation), data analysis, and personalized recommendations.
- **Types**:
  - **Reactive Agents**: Respond to immediate stimuli without memory.
  - **Deliberative Agents**: Use models or planning to achieve goals.
  - **Hybrid Agents**: Combine reactive and deliberative approaches.
- **Challenges**: Include ethical concerns, bias mitigation, transparency, and alignment with human values.

Ai-Agents are frequently deployed as fundamental units within **multi-agent systems (MAS)**, where multiple agents interact—either cooperatively or competitively—to address problems beyond the capability of a single agent. In these frameworks, coordination mechanisms, communication protocols, and negotiation strategies are essential for managing agent interactions and achieving system-level objectives.
